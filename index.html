My Story

The final over of a close cricket match has always felt similar to solving a difficult coding problem. In both situations, everything comes down to focus, clarity, and making the right decision under pressure. Growing up playing cricket, this way of thinking became natural to me even before I started coding. I started playing cricket seriously and was given the responsibility of captaining my team at the age of thirteen. At first, I thought leadership meant giving instructions and making decisions. But slowly, I understood that it is more about taking responsibility, especially when things are not going well. A captain has to stay calm under pressure and bring the team together towards one goal. I still remember situations where matches were slipping away, and the focus shifted from individual performance to how the team could still function effectively. This experience taught me discipline, patience, and accountability, which reflects in the way I approach my studies. My interest in computer science began when I started exploring coding out of curiosity. At first, it was experimentation, but later it became structured learning. I built two Python-based projects related to cricket analytics—a match win predictor and a player performance analyzer. I used datasets from Kaggle and applied logistic regression using Scikit-learn to make predictions based on historical data. Through this, I realized that model performance depends less on the algorithm and more on how well the data is prepared. There were moments when results were inconsistent, and I had to revisit feature selection and data cleaning multiple times before the model worked properly. I also used Pandas and Matplotlib for analysis and Streamlit to create a simple interface. Uploading my work on GitHub helped me refine and structure my projects step by step. What I enjoyed most was not just the final output, but breaking a complex problem into smaller parts—data cleaning, feature selection, testing models, and improving results. It felt similar to cricket, where you focus on one ball at a time instead of the entire match. The pandemic was a difficult phase in my academic life. Like many students, I struggled with online learning and lost consistency. My performance dropped, and it took time to understand the gap between effort and outcome. Slowly, I rebuilt my discipline and took responsibility for my routine. This helped me improve my academic performance, and I scored 88% in my 10th-grade boards. Since then, I have stayed more consistent and I am currently aiming for above 90% in my 12th-grade PCM stream. Coming from a middle-class family in Karnataka has helped me stay grounded and value education. My father works in banking and finance, and my mother is a homemaker. Their support has played an important role in my journey. While I understand stability, I have realized that I enjoy solving logical problems and building systems that work. Computer science feels right because it combines logic, creativity, and persistence. I am particularly interested in Computer Science and Engineering, especially Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning. These fields interest me because they allow systems to learn from data and improve over time. I am motivated by turning data into meaningful decisions and improving systems continuously. I want to study in an environment that challenges me and helps me strengthen my problem-solving ability. Just like the final over of a cricket match, where every decision matters and clarity under pressure is crucial, I see computer science in the same way—requiring focus, logic, and continuous improvement. I hope to pursue Computer Science and Engineering at your university, where I can build my technical skills and grow into someone who can design systems that solve meaningful real-world problems.
